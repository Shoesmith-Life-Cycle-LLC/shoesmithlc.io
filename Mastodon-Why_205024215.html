Why move to Mastodon

Mastodon is a free, open-source social media platform that is based on open web protocols and decentralized technology. The idea behind Mastodon and other free and open-source software (FOSS) is that anyone can freely use, modify, and distribute the software, as long as they share their modifications with the community. This approach allows for a more collaborative and transparent development process, and it ensures that the software remains freely available for everyone to use.There are several reasons why FOSS is considered the way of the future by many people:
Collaborative development: FOSS allows for a more collaborative and transparent development process, as anyone can contribute to the project and see the changes that are being made.
Freedom to use and modify: With FOSS, users have the freedom to use the software in any way they see fit, and to modify it to meet their specific needs.
No vendor lock-in: FOSS allows users to avoid being locked into a single vendor, as they have the freedom to switch to another solution if they wish.
Increased security: FOSS allows users to review the source code, which can help to identify and fix potential security vulnerabilities.
Community support: FOSS projects often have a large and active community of users and developers who can provide support and help troubleshoot issues.
Overall, FOSS is considered the way of the future by many people because it promotes collaboration, freedom, and transparency, and it offers a number of benefits for users and developers. 

Mastodon Founder & C.E.O. Eugen Rochko

"Eugen Rochko, the 29-year-old German software developer who six years ago created Twitter rival Mastodon out of frustration with the U.S. social network, has had quite a month. The chaos of Elon Musk’s Twitter takeover in late October has led millions of users to look for an alternative, and Mastodon has provided many with a familiar—though not identical—life raft. The influx of users is fueling Rochko’s ambition of Mastodon one day matching Twitter in size, but the founder and CEO is taking a decidedly anti-Musk approach to growing his decentralized platform—forbidding hate speech, banning ads, eschewing revenue, and abdicating hands-on control of the network. He remains Mastodon’s sole full-time employee."
